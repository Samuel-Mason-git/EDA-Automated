Skewness
Issues

If your dataset is skewed, it might impact your analysis or modeling in important ways:

  • You’re using models that assume normally distributed data (e.g., linear regression, logistic regression, ANOVA)
  • You rely on mean or standard deviation for detecting outliers or scaling
  • Your dataset is small, so extreme values have more influence
  • Your model’s performance is unexpectedly poor or predictions seem biased

Skewness indicates asymmetry in the distribution of a dataset, which can negatively impact many statistical models and machine learning algorithms that assume normality.

Your dataset contains {{ data_quality_recommendations['cardinality_recommendations']['medium_cardinality_columns'] | length }} Medium Cardinality columns.

{% endif %}

High cardinality in a dataset occurs when a column has a large number of unique values, which can lead to increased computational costs and slower model performance. It also risks overfitting, as the model may focus too heavily on rare or irrelevant values, rather than identifying generalisable patterns that contribute to more accurate predictions. One-Hot-Encoding a categorical column with High or Medium Cardinality could also have a large impact on the dimensionality of the data.

💡 Tip: Drop columns that have a high cardinality and no predictive power (e.g., IDs, URLs, or any column with random/unimportant data).

Tips for Handling High Cardinality:
  • Consider grouping or binning rare categories to reduce the number of unique values.
  • Explore feature engineering to categorise into higher level categories to reduce unique value count.
  • Use target encoding or hashing to transform categorical values into numeric ones for efficient processing.
  • Explore dimensionality reduction techniques like PCA or feature hashing to reduce feature complexity.

 Feature Engineering
Feature Engineering Guidance
Info
Remember to Feature Engineer your data!

Transform raw data into meaningful signals by understanding relationships and patterns.

Foundational Techniques:
  • Combinations: Multiply/divide related features
  • Decomposition: Extract day/month/year from dates
  • Binning: Group continuous variables
  • Aggregations: Category-level statistics
Advanced Approaches:
  • Target Encoding: Encode categories by target
  • Feature Learning: Autoencoders/embeddings
  • Domain Features: Business-specific ratios
  • Cross-Features: Interaction terms
Key Considerations:
  • Prioritise interpretable features where possible
  • Validate with train/test splits to avoid leakage
  • Monitor feature performance in production
  • Balance creativity with maintainability

💡 Good features should make patterns obvious to simple models.
